Wes Craven

The iconic horror film director known for A Nightmare on Elm Street and Scream

Here is a timeline of Craven's notable film releases:

  • 1972 - Wrote and directed "The Last House on the Left" which follows two teenage girls who are terrorized by unexpected company.
  • 1977 - Wrote, directed and edited "The Hills Have Eyes" which follows a suburban family that is targeted by a group of cannibalistic savages in the Nevada desert.
  • 1984 - Wrote and directed "A Nightmare on Elm Street" which follows teenagers who are terrorized by the now iconic entity known as Freddy Krueger. The movie is considered one of the best horror films of all time.
  • 1996 - Directed the first installment of "Scream" which follows high school students stalked and killed by a masked knife wieding psycho. The satirical film was considered unique at the time of its release and would go on to earn $173 million, becoming the highest-grossing slasher film of all time. The cult classic was also credited with reviving the horror genre in the 90s decade as it was starting to die off, no pun intended.
  • 1997 - Directed and produced "Scream 2" as the sequel to the successful first film. The movie was a box office success and priased by critics as surpassing the original in quality.
  • 2000 - Directed "Scream 3" which followed the protagonist from the previous two films as she tries to stop the masked killer once again.
  • 2005 Directed "Red Eye" which follows a hotel manager onboard a flight with an assassin. The film was met with mostly postitive reviews from critics.
  • 2007 Wrote and produced "The Hills Have Eyes 2" the sequel to the 1977 film which also served as a remake. It followed US Army National Guardsmen as they fight for survival against the cannibalistic threat on a New Mexico military base.
  • 2011 - Directed and produced his final film "Scream 4" which continued over a decade after the last.
  • 2015 - Craven passes away at the age of 78 due to a brain tumor. He will be remembered by the horror commmunity as one of the creative visionaries with several classics under his belt.
